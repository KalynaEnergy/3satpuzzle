Reading a circle. The digit never changes — it's what the rule demands of that switch (1 = wants it ON, 0 = wants it OFF). The colour is live: red means the switch doesn't currently meet that demand, green means it does, and bright green means this cell is the only reason its row is satisfied right now (flip that switch and the row breaks). A tiny dot means the rule doesn't care about that switch. Use ◀▶ under a switch to reorder columns; with 6+ switches, Right‑3 +1 steps the three rightmost columns through all eight ON/OFF combinations like a binary counter — reorder first to park the switches you want to brute‑force on the right.

3‑SAT. Switches are variables, a circle is a literal, a row is a 3‑literal clause, the board is their AND. Every puzzle has exactly one solution, planted then fenced in by rules until nothing else survives.
